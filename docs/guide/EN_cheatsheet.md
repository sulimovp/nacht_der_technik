## The 3 big ideas (the whole stand in 3 lines)
1. **Superposition** — a quantum thing can be in two states at once (spinning coin).
2. **Measurement** — looking forces it to pick one, at **random**; you can't predict which.
3. **Quantum randomness is a resource** — truly unpredictable numbers power security, AI, new computers.

## 30-second pitches
- **Hack the Light:** "Turn light into a 0/1. First you control it, then a hidden rule, then pure noise
  you can't steer — that's how we harvest randomness."
- **Beat the AI:** "Tap left/right; the AI predicts you and wins. Humans can't be random — that's why
  we need quantum randomness."
- **Quantum Penalty:** "Beat a mind-reading keeper. The quantum shot is in two corners at once and only
  'decides' when it arrives — he can't read what isn't decided yet."
- **Quantum Chess:** "Chess where a piece can be in two squares at once (superposition), get entangled,
  and 'collapse' to one square when challenged."
- **Quantum Tic-Tac-Toe:** "Your mark goes in two squares at once; tangled marks collapse into real
  ones. Superposition + entanglement + measurement in 30 seconds."

## Honesty (if asked "is this *really* quantum?")
- Be proud and honest: **these are quantum-*inspired* games and a *physical* random source** (camera
  noise), not quantum hardware. They build the *right intuition*; the real tech uses single photons.
- Never claim there's a quantum computer or single-photon device on the stand.

## Top FAQ
- *"What is quantum, simply?"* → "The rules for the tiniest things: they can be in two states at once,
  and looking forces a random choice nobody can predict."
- *"Why does randomness matter?"* → "Every password and secure chat needs unpredictable keys. Predictable
  = hackable. Quantum gives the most unpredictable numbers there are."
- *"Can entanglement send messages faster than light?"* → "No — it's a correlation, not a phone."
- *"Is a quantum computer just faster?"* → "Different, and only faster for certain problems."
- *"How do I beat the AI / quantum keeper?"* → "Be truly random — which humans basically can't be."
